Introduction - Basic information about Miami

In this article I will be covering some of the basic information everybody should know about the capital of the Sunshine State in the United States. I will talk about Miami’s historical development, its location and geographical position, climate, demographic profile, economy, culture and cuisine as well as some of the most popular spots to visit in Miami.

In order to get a grasp of aspects of Miami I mentioned in the paragraph above, we first need to get an overview of some of the basic information about Miami:



Miami or officially the City of Miami is the capital of Florida state in the United States of America. Miami is the financial, economic and cultural center of South Florida. The area of the city is approximately 150 square kilometers. Miami is the sixth most densely populated city in the United States with its population reaching 500 thousand residents.

Miami is a major center and leader in commerce, culture, arts, finance and international trade. The Miami metropolitan area is the seventh largest in the US, as well as the largest urban economy in Florida and the 12th largest in the US. Miami is ranked seventh in the United States in terms of human capital, information exchange, business activity, cultural experience and political engagement. The City of Miami is ranked as the third richest city in the United States. Miami is nicknamed the “Capital of Latin America” as it is the largest city with a Cuban-American plurality.

Downtown Miami is home to one of the largest concentrations of international banks and national and international companies. Miami’s so called “Health District” is a major center for clinics, hospitals, biotechnological and medical research industries. Port of Miami is the busiest cruise port in the world in both passenger traffic and cruise lines. It’s commonly known as the “Cruise Capital of the World”. Miami is the second most famous tourism hub for international visitors in the US, ranking after New York.
